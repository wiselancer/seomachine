# Semen Petrenko (petrenko.cv) — Brand Voice

## Voice: Builder Who Ships

### Core Persona
Semen is a builder. He doesn't just consult — he ships. His personal brand should feel like a senior engineer's blog: precise, opinionated, practical, no fluff. He has receipts — products in production, years of shipped code.

### Tone Pillars

#### 1. Show Don't Tell
- Not: "I'm an experienced developer"
- Yes: "I've shipped 7 production apps including a B2B SaaS with paying users (LeadCognition) and a dev agency with clients since 2011 (Fruitful Code)"

#### 2. Builder's Directness
- Skip the career-page polish. Be direct.
- "Here's what I've built, how I built it, what I'd do differently."
- Opinions are welcome. Hedging is not.

#### 3. Technical Credibility
- References real technologies correctly (Bun not Node, Hono not Express, Drizzle not Prisma)
- Makes technical decisions and explains why
- Knows the tradeoffs

#### 4. Ukrainian Builder in the Global Market
- Proud of the work, unashamed of location
- Fruitful Code = 10+ years of delivered projects from Kyiv
- Context: building during war, maintaining quality, shipping anyway

### Writing Style
- First person throughout (I built, I shipped, I learned)
- Short paragraphs — this is a developer's blog, not corporate copy
- Code examples where relevant
- Specific numbers: "3 weeks to MVP", "paying customers day 1", "6 production apps"

### Content That Works for Personal Brand
1. **Building in public** — "What I learned building X"
2. **Technical opinionated posts** — "Why I use Bun over Node in 2026"
3. **Product postmortems** — honest lessons from each build
4. **Consulting/services page** — "Work with me" with specific engagement models

## Quality Check
- [ ] Reads like a person wrote it, not a PR agency
- [ ] Includes specific technical details
- [ ] Has an opinion or a stance
- [ ] Would a developer find this interesting?
