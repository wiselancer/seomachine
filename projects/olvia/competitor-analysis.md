# OLVIA Group Competitor Analysis

## Competitive Landscape

Olvia competes in the global oilseed processing and export market. Buyers compare suppliers on: capacity, specs (protein/FFA), price basis (FOB/CIF), reliability, and certifications.

## Primary Competitor Types

### Large Ukrainian Agri-Industrial Groups
- **Kernel (Kernel.ua)**: Largest Ukrainian sunflower oil producer, publicly traded. Volume advantage but less flexible for mid-size buyers.
- **Bunge Ukraine, Cargill Ukraine, ADM**: Global commodity traders with Ukrainian operations. Buyers know these brands but prefer local producers for direct pricing.
- **OLVIA's angle**: Direct producer access (no trader margin), smaller minimum orders possible, long-term relationship focus vs. commodity spot market

### Other Ukrainian Oilseed Processors
- Mid-size extraction plants in Mykolaiv, Kherson, Zaporizhzhia regions
- **OLVIA's angle**: Modern equipment (European standard), 84,700 m³ storage (supply reliability), 178 km to port (logistics cost), waste-free process (biofuel upcycling)

### Eastern European + Black Sea Competitors
- Romania, Bulgaria, Russian (pre-2022) sunflower oil exporters
- **OLVIA's angle**: Ukrainian sunflower quality is globally recognized as highest grade; direct EU neighbor logistics

## Content Angles from Competitive Positioning

1. **"Why Source Sunflower Oil Directly from a Ukrainian Processor?"** — cut out the trader markup; spec control; relationship pricing
2. **"Sunflower Meal Protein Specs: What Buyers Need to Know"** — educational, positions OLVIA as knowledgeable supplier
3. **"Ukraine Sunflower Oil: Quality, Capacity, and Export Logistics Explained"** — authority hub page for procurement buyers
4. **"CIF vs FOB Odessa: What's the Right Delivery Term for Your Business?"** — extremely practical, earns trust from procurement managers
5. **"Sunflower Husk Pellets: The Biofuel Byproduct Most Processors Waste"** — differentiator piece on their waste-free model

## What Buyers Actually Compare (address these in content)
- Protein % in meal (35–38% sunflower vs 44–46% soy — explain tradeoffs)
- FFA (free fatty acid) levels in crude oil — quality indicator
- Delivery reliability — buffer stock, lead times
- Certifications — EU standards, ISCC for biofuel, GAFTA for trading
- Logistics — port proximity, rail vs. road, container vs. bulk tanker
- Payment terms — LC, TT, open account for established buyers
