# OLVIA Group Brand Voice & Messaging

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Industrial Authority
- **What it means**: Buyers of bulk commodities make six-figure purchasing decisions. They need hard numbers and certified quality — not marketing copy.
- **How it sounds**: Factual, capacity-forward, specification-precise. Like a reliable supplier's product datasheet that also explains why they're the right partner.
- **Example**: "1,200 tons per day processing capacity. 84,700 m³ elevator storage. Two-month buffer inventory. Your supply chain doesn't stop when we're at capacity."
- **Avoid**: "premium quality" without specs, "world-class" without evidence, lifestyle language

### 2. Ukrainian Resilience
- **What it means**: Operating from Ukraine in 2024–2026 is a statement of operational stability. Buyers need to know supply is reliable despite regional challenges.
- **How it sounds**: Factual confidence. Not defensive, not dramatic — just "we're here, we're shipping, here are the numbers."
- **Example**: "178 km from Odesa — one of Europe's most important grain and oilseed export corridors. We've maintained continuous production and export since founding."
- **Avoid**: Dwelling on conflict; let operational facts speak

### 3. Transparent Specifications
- **What it means**: Industrial buyers run RFQs (requests for quote) based on protein content, moisture, FFA, delivery terms. We lead with specs.
- **How it sounds**: Direct, technical, Incoterms-aware
- **Example**: "Sunflower meal: 35–38% protein, available CIF, FOB Odesa, or FCA Berizky. Minimum order quantities on request."
- **Avoid**: Vague "high-quality products" without measurable specs

### 4. Long-Term Partnership
- **What it means**: Commodity buyers need reliable multi-year supply relationships, not spot deals
- **How it sounds**: Relationship-oriented, reliability-focused
- **Example**: "50+ countries trust OLVIA as a consistent supplier — not because of lowest price, but because our lead times and quality are predictable."
- **Avoid**: Promotional urgency, discount language

## Tone by Content Type

**Product Pages**: Technical specs first, then context
- "Crude Sunflower Oil — FFA ≤0.5%, moisture ≤0.1%, available in bulk tanker or ISO containers"

**Company/About Content**: Capacity + logistics + stability
- Numbers-forward: "400,000 tons/year. 84,700 m³ storage. 178 km to port."

**News/Market Content**: Industry-relevant, factual
- Sunflower harvest outlook, Ukrainian export volumes, seasonal availability
- Links to USDA/FAO/GAFTA data for credibility

**Export Market Content** (for international SEO):
- Written for procurement managers who are running supplier comparisons
- Answer: MOQ, quality specs, delivery terms, certifications

## Target Buyer Persona

**International Procurement Manager**:
- Based in EU, GCC, Southeast Asia
- Evaluating 3–5 sunflower oil or meal suppliers
- Needs: protein specs, FFA levels, ISCC/EU certification confirmation, logistics details, pricing basis
- Searches: "sunflower oil bulk supplier ukraine", "sunflower meal 36% protein supplier", "ukraine soybean meal exporter"

## Value Propositions

**For oil refiners**: "400,000 tons/year crude sunflower, soy, and rapeseed oil from a single vertically-integrated source. Consistent FFA specs, direct port access."

**For feed manufacturers**: "Sunflower meal at 35–38% protein and soy meal at 44–46% protein — produced on-site from own extraction, not blended."

**For energy companies**: "Pelleted sunflower husk biofuel — zero-waste byproduct of our extraction process. Consistent calorific value, available in bulk."
