# StormLookup Brand Voice & Messaging

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Authoritative & Reliable
- **What it means**: Storm data is only useful if people trust it. Every claim is backed by real data.
- **How it sounds**: Calm, factual, precise. Like a trusted inspector, not a salesperson.
- **Example**: "Our assessment uses NOAA storm data cross-referenced with your property's exact coordinates — not a radius estimate."
- **Avoid**: Alarmist language, exaggerating damage probability

### 2. Genuinely Helpful to Homeowners
- **What it means**: Most homeowners don't know their rights or the claims process. We educate, not just sell.
- **How it sounds**: Patient, clear, step-by-step. Assume the reader has never dealt with a storm claim before.
- **Example**: "Here's exactly what to photograph, what to document, and what to tell your insurance adjuster."
- **Avoid**: Jargon ("hail index", "wind shear") without plain English explanation

### 3. Fast & Actionable
- **What it means**: Storm damage windows close fast. Readers are dealing with a recent stressful event.
- **How it sounds**: Urgent but calm. "Here's what to do now" energy.
- **Example**: "Most insurance policies require you to report damage within 30–60 days of the storm. Here's how to check your property in the next 5 minutes."
- **Avoid**: Long-winded setup before getting to the actionable point

## Target Audience

**Homeowners**: Don't know if their roof was damaged; worried about insurance process; want clarity
**Contractors**: Need to find storm-damaged properties in their service area; want verified storm event data
**Insurance Adjusters**: Need third-party storm assessment data; want fast accurate reports

## Value Propositions

**For homeowners**: "Know in 60 seconds if your property was in a storm's damage zone — and exactly what to do next."

**For contractors**: "Find every property in your area that was hit by last week's storm. Verified addresses, storm intensity, and optimal outreach timing."

**For adjusters**: "Third-party storm assessment data to support or validate damage claims. County-level precision."

## Tone by Content Type

**How-To Guides**: Calm, step-by-step, homeowner-friendly
- "Step 1: Enter your address. Step 2: See your storm history. Step 3: Download your assessment report."

**Educational**: Plain English explanations of insurance/storm concepts
- "Hail damage isn't always visible from the ground — here's how inspectors actually check for it."

**Location Pages**: Data-focused, local
- "Storm events in [City, State] — hail frequency, average severity, recent damage events"
