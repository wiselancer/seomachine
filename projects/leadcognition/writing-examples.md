# LeadCognition Writing Examples

## Excellent LeadCognition Voice ✅

**Example 1 — Blog intro (developer outreach)**

"Most cold emails to developers get ignored — not because developers hate email, but because the message has zero relevance to what they're actually doing.

Sending 'I noticed your company might need [tool]' to a developer who starred your competitor's repo yesterday is the difference between a 2% reply rate and a 15% reply rate. One message is a guess. The other is based on something they did.

That's what GitHub signals are for. Here's exactly how to use them."

**Why this works**:
- Leads with the problem, not a feature list
- Specific numbers (2% vs 15%) — not "much better"
- Technical but plain-spoken
- Ends with a promise, not a sales pitch

---

**Example 2 — Comparison section (vs Apollo)**

"Apollo gives you 200 million contacts. Roughly 150 million of them are irrelevant to a developer-tool company.

The problem isn't data volume — it's signal. When you're selling to engineers, job title filters tell you what someone's paid to do, not what they're actually evaluating right now. A senior backend engineer at a fintech who starred three infrastructure repos in the last week is a better lead than any Apollo filter can surface.

LeadCognition starts where Apollo ends: after you've found the person, we tell you why now."

**Why this works**:
- Specific numbers make the comparison concrete
- Doesn't trash Apollo — frames the category gap
- Technical framing ("signal", "evaluating") resonates with dev-tool buyers
- Punchy closing line

---

**Example 3 — CTA**

"See which developers starred your competitor's GitHub repos this week — and reach them before anyone else does. [Start free →]"

**Why this works**: Specific, active, creates urgency without being fake-urgent

## Not LeadCognition Voice ❌

**Bad Example 1 — Too corporate**

"LeadCognition is a powerful, comprehensive B2B SaaS platform that leverages cutting-edge GitHub data to provide robust developer intelligence solutions for sales teams looking to optimize their outbound pipeline generation efforts and maximize ROI on prospecting activities."

**Why this fails**: Jargon soup. Zero specificity. "Powerful/robust/comprehensive" = says nothing.

---

**Bad Example 2 — Too hype-y**

"Stop struggling with old-school prospecting and transform your pipeline with 10x better developer leads. Our revolutionary AI-powered platform makes finding developers effortless and supercharges your entire GTM motion!"

**Why this fails**: "Revolutionary", "10x", "supercharges" — developers can smell this from a mile away and immediately distrust the source.

---

**Bad Example 3 — Too timid**

"LeadCognition might potentially be a useful option to consider for companies that are perhaps looking for some ways to possibly improve their developer outreach efforts in certain situations."

**Why this fails**: No conviction, no specificity, no value.
