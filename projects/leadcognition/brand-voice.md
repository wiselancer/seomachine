# LeadCognition Brand Voice & Messaging

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Signal Over Noise
- **What it means**: Developers are drowning in irrelevant outreach. LeadCognition exists to cut through with real data — actual GitHub activity, real intent, verifiable signals.
- **How it sounds**: Specific, data-driven, no vague promises. Name the signal. Name the outcome.
- **Example**: "When a developer stars your competitor's repo, that's not a vague 'interest signal' — it's a purchase consideration event. LeadCognition captures it."
- **Avoid**: "powerful insights", "deep data", "comprehensive platform" — empty superlatives

### 2. Developer-First Language
- **What it means**: Our users and our targets are both technical. We don't translate down. We speak GitHub, not Salesforce.
- **How it sounds**: Uses correct technical terms (repos, commits, stars, PRs, issues). References real developer workflows. No sales jargon.
- **Example**: "Filter by developers who opened issues on aws-cdk in the last 30 days — those are your infra buyers."
- **Avoid**: "leads", "prospects" (say "developers"), "synergies", "leverage"

### 3. Precision Over Hype
- **What it means**: Developers can smell marketing BS. Every claim should be verifiable or demonstrable.
- **How it sounds**: Measured, confident, specific numbers. Acknowledges limitations honestly.
- **Example**: "We surface the 3% of GitHub activity that's actually a buying signal — not all 100M events."
- **Avoid**: "game-changing", "revolutionary", "10x your pipeline" without evidence

### 4. Respect Their Time
- **What it means**: Our audience is busy engineers and founders. Content should get to the point fast.
- **How it sounds**: Direct. Bottom line up front. Short paragraphs. No padding.
- **Example**: "TL;DR: GitHub stars = commercial intent. Here's how to turn them into pipeline."
- **Avoid**: Long intros, throat-clearing, "In today's competitive landscape..."

## Tone Guidelines

### General Tone
Imagine you're a senior B2B growth engineer briefing a smart colleague before a call. You know your data cold, you're not overselling anything, and you cut straight to what's actionable. You're helpful, not pushy. You assume the reader is technical enough to follow along.

### Tone by Content Type

**How-To Guides**: Direct, step-by-step, technical
- "Here's the exact filter setup we use to find infra buyers on GitHub"
- "Run this query to pull developers who starred X in the last 14 days"
- "The output is a CSV — import it directly into your sequencer"

**Strategy/Opinion Content**: Confident, analytical, contrarian where warranted
- "Most developer outreach fails because it's based on job titles, not behavior"
- "The conventional wisdom on cold email to engineers is wrong — here's the data"

**Comparison/Alternative Content**: Fair, specific, outcome-focused
- "Apollo has 200M contacts. LeadCognition has 2M developers — and we know what they built yesterday."
- "Clay is powerful but requires 20 hours of setup. LeadCognition is domain → contacts in 60 seconds."

**Product/Feature Content**: Benefit-led, not feature-led
- "You stop guessing and start knowing who's actively evaluating infrastructure tools"
- "Every contact unlock comes with the signal that triggered it — so your first message is actually relevant"

## Messaging Framework

### Core Messages

#### 1. Intent Data for Developers
- **Concept**: GitHub activity is the highest-quality intent signal for developer-tool companies
- **Key Points**: Stars = purchase consideration; Issues = active evaluation; Commits = technical fit; PRs = collaboration interest
- **Usage**: All outbound-focused content, top-of-funnel awareness

#### 2. From GitHub to Inbox in Minutes
- **Concept**: LeadCognition collapses the workflow from "who to reach" to "how to reach them" into one platform
- **Key Points**: No need for 5 separate tools; domain → contacts pipeline; enriched with LinkedIn + email
- **Usage**: Product pages, comparison content, onboarding

#### 3. Built for Dev-Tool GTM Teams
- **Concept**: Generic B2B tools weren't built for the developer-tools market and it shows
- **Key Points**: Apollo has no GitHub layer; Hunter.io has no intent data; Clay requires engineering resources
- **Usage**: Competitor comparison pages, alternative pages

### Value Propositions

**For SDRs/BDRs**: "Stop cold-emailing developers who've never heard of your product. Start with the ones who starred your competitor's repo last week."

**For Founders doing outbound**: "Skip the 5-tool stack. Drop in a domain, get a list of developers actively evaluating tools in your category."

**For GTM engineers**: "GitHub activity → enriched contact data → personalized outreach, fully automated. One API call."

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Sentence Structure
- **Short and punchy**: 10–20 words per sentence ideal
- **Active voice always**: "LeadCognition surfaces" not "Signals are surfaced by LeadCognition"
- **Lead with the insight**: Don't bury the point
- **Use em-dashes for impact**: "That's not a lead — it's a purchase signal."

### Word Choice
- **Say**: developer, signal, intent, enrich, unlock, pipeline, outbound, workflow
- **Avoid**: synergy, leverage (as verb), utilize, solution, empower, robust, seamless
- **Numbers beat adjectives**: "214 developers starred your competitor in 30 days" > "many developers"

### Terminology Preferences
- **"Developers"** → not "leads" or "prospects"
- **"Signals"** → not "insights" or "data points"
- **"Unlock"** → not "purchase" or "buy" (for credits)
- **"Outreach"** → not "spam" and not "demand generation"
- **"GitHub activity"** → not "social signals" or "online behavior"

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any LeadCognition content:
- [ ] Passes the "developer sniff test" — would a senior SWE find this cringeworthy?
- [ ] At least one specific, concrete data example or workflow
- [ ] No empty superlatives (powerful, robust, comprehensive)
- [ ] CTA is specific — "start a free trial", "see pricing", not "learn more"
- [ ] Mentions at least one real use case or persona
