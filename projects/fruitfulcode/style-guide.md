# FruitfulCode Style Guide

## Tone and Readability

- Write in clear, direct English for founders, startup operators, and technical decision-makers
- Use technical terms when they improve precision, but explain why a decision matters in business terms
- Prefer short paragraphs and concrete examples over abstract claims
- Sound like an experienced delivery partner, not a sales brochure

## Voice Rules

- Lead with outcomes, then explain the technical reasoning
- Use specifics instead of vague modifiers
- Be consultative and confident without sounding pushy
- Frame Fruitful Code as a partner that advises and ships, not a passive execution vendor

## Preferred Language

Say:
- custom web development
- mobile app development
- MVP
- product team
- delivery partner
- ongoing support
- product roadmap
- technical discovery
- real estate platform
- startup build

Avoid:
- cheap
- affordable
- low-cost
- revolutionary
- cutting-edge
- robust solution
- seamless experience
- one-stop shop
- digital transformation partner

## Formatting

### Headlines
- Use title case for H1 and H2
- Use sentence case for H3 and below when it reads more naturally
- Keep headlines specific and tied to the query intent
- For time-sensitive content, include the year when it strengthens search intent

### Paragraphs
- Keep most paragraphs to 2-4 sentences
- Break up dense sections with bullets, tables, and subheads
- Use bold sparingly for emphasis on decisions, outcomes, and numbers

### Numbers and Proof
- Use digits for timelines, percentages, prices, and counts
- Prefer concrete phrasing: "12-16 weeks", "10+ years", "20+ projects a year"
- Whenever possible, pair claims with a delivery example or business outcome

## CTA Style

Preferred CTA language:
- "Get a free estimate"
- "Talk to our team"
- "Book a discovery call"
- "See if we're a fit"

Avoid:
- "Click here"
- "Contact us today" without context
- "Start now" without a clear next step

## Article Structure

Standard service or educational article:
1. Direct answer in the opening lines
2. Clear problem framing
3. Key takeaways block
4. Main body with one idea per section
5. Practical next steps
6. Soft but specific CTA

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Mentions a concrete business outcome or delivery benefit
- [ ] Includes at least one specific number, timeframe, or example
- [ ] Avoids low-price positioning
- [ ] Reads like an experienced team speaking to a serious buyer
- [ ] CTA invites a conversation, not a hard sell
