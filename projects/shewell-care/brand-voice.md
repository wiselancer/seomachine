# Shewell.care Brand Voice & Messaging

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Warmth with Clinical Authority
- **What it means**: Women's health is personal and often anxiety-inducing. We balance clinical expertise with genuine warmth.
- **How it sounds**: Calm, reassuring, knowledgeable. Like a senior consultant who takes time with you.
- **Example**: "Whether you're coming to us with a specific concern or just for a routine check, we make sure you leave with clarity — not more questions."
- **Avoid**: Cold clinical language OR overly casual tone that undermines expertise

### 2. No Dismissal, No Minimization
- **What it means**: Women are frequently dismissed in healthcare. We explicitly acknowledge this and position Shewell as the antidote.
- **How it sounds**: Validating, listening-oriented, takes concerns seriously
- **Example**: "Your symptoms are real, and you deserve an answer. We don't wait for things to get worse before we investigate."
- **Avoid**: "common", "normal", "nothing to worry about" without explanation

### 3. Privacy & Discretion
- **What it means**: Private healthcare means confidentiality. Our audience values privacy above all.
- **How it sounds**: Understated, trust-building, never sensational
- **Example**: "Everything discussed in your consultation stays between you and your specialist."
- **Avoid**: Over-sharing patient scenarios, even in anonymized form

### 4. Empowerment Through Information
- **What it means**: Informed patients make better decisions and have better outcomes. We educate, not gatekeep.
- **How it sounds**: Explains options clearly, gives context for recommendations
- **Example**: "Here are the three most common approaches for managing PCOS — we'll discuss which fits your goals and lifestyle."
- **Avoid**: Jargon-heavy medical language without plain English translation

## Tone by Content Type

**Educational Articles**: Clear, accessible, backed by evidence
- "What HRT options are available and how to choose the right one"
- "The difference between PCOS and endometriosis — and why it matters for treatment"

**Service Pages**: Warm, professional, outcomes-focused
- "Our fertility specialists take a full picture of your health before recommending any diagnostic tests."

**FAQ Content**: Conversational, direct, reassuring
- "It's completely normal to feel nervous about your first private gynecology appointment. Here's exactly what to expect."

## Value Propositions

**For general gynecology**: "A specialist who listens, investigates, and actually explains what's happening — not a 7-minute GP appointment."

**For fertility**: "From initial consultation to a personalised fertility plan — including the tests that matter and options that fit your timeline."

**For menopause**: "Expert menopause management, including HRT options, explained clearly and tailored to your body and symptoms."

## Tone Rules
- Always use "your specialist" or "our team" — not generic "the doctor"
- Avoid: "sufferers", "patients" (prefer "women", "you")
- Preferred: "specialist", "consultant", "personalised", "private consultation"
- UK English: personalised (not personalized), gynaecology (not gynecology) in content
- Never dramatize — health content should reduce anxiety, not increase it
